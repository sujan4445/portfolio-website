Agriculture Undergraduate and Research Scholar

Mr. Sujan is an undergraduate student in agriculture at the Institute of Agriculture and Animal Science in Lamjung. His research focuses on critical areas impacting agricultural productivity, including crop characterization, phenotyping under abiotic stress conditions, nutrient management strategies, and broader plant science topics. By exploring innovative approaches in plant physiology and agronomy, he aims to contribute meaningfully to sustainable agriculture practices.

Research

I have worked extensively on research projects focused on improving crop resilience and productivity. My studies include the performance evaluation of wheat genotypes under drought and irrigated conditions, agromorphological characterization of Nepalese finger millet landraces, and the assessment of NDVI and canopy temperature using precision agriculture tools. These projects aim to generate actionable insights for breeders, agronomists, and policymakers to foster sustainable and climate-resilient agricultural systems.
